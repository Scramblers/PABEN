# Universal Intelligence as Repeatable Displacement

A PABEN Study on Regulation, Continuity and Physical Organization


## Abstract

This study proposes a cautious interpretation of intelligence as *repeatable continuity-preserving displacement*. The claim is not that physical systems are intelligent in the human or cognitive sense. The claim is more limited: across several layers of physical organization, stable systems appear to arise when a field or system can repeatedly transform, exchange or displace something without losing the continuity that makes the system identifiable and regulable.

In PABEN terms, the hypothesis is that a stable physical system can be read as an organism-like regulatory structure: a continuity pressure is met by a repeatable regulator-skill within a context, producing retained relations that constrain future possibilities. This interpretation is speculative. It is offered as an observation pattern and a conceptual translation, not as an established physical theory.

The study is related to several existing traditions, including non-equilibrium thermodynamics, autopoiesis, relational quantum mechanics, the free energy principle, autocatalytic set theory and information-based approaches to physics. These frameworks do not imply PABEN, and PABEN does not claim to replace them. They do, however, show that relation, regulation, boundary maintenance, physical information and self-maintaining organization are legitimate scientific themes.

---

## 1. Framing

PABEN describes intelligence and agency through five domains: Perception / Contexts, Action / Skills, Beliefs, Experience and Needs. In its biological form, the model treats a Belief as an action-context commitment: *do Skill while Context*. Experience retains successful transitions, and Needs supply the pressure for continuation.

This study asks whether the same structure can be used as a speculative lens on physical organization. The proposed translation is not psychological. A proton, atom or field does not have thoughts, intentions or representations. The question is whether such systems can be described structurally as continuity-preserving regulatory arrangements.

The guiding observation is:

> Stable organization appears where possible transformations become repeatable enough to preserve a continuity.

The guiding hypothesis is:

> Intelligence, in its most minimal and non-psychological form, may be understood as the conversion of unstable possibility into usable continuation through repeatable displacement.

---

## 2. Core Observation

A continuity is not preserved by standing still. It is preserved by being carried through change. A system remains itself only if some relation can be displaced, transformed, exchanged, measured, propagated or reconfigured without destroying the regulatory identity of the system.

Examples can be stated cautiously:

- Space-time stability depends on repeatable causal and metric relations.
- Matter stability depends on energy becoming locally persistent.
- Hadronic stability depends on color charge being internally exchanged while remaining externally confined.
- Atomic stability depends on electromagnetic charge relations forming repeatable bound states.
- Biological stability depends on organism-environment exchange occurring without loss of organismic closure.

The shared structure is not substance, but regulation:

```text
unstable field or system
-> attempted displacement
-> repeatable continuity
-> retained relation
-> available continuation
-> regulated inside/outside
-> stable organism-like structure
```

In this study, *organism-like* means only that a system maintains an inside/outside distinction through repeatable regulation. It does not imply life, consciousness or subjective experience.

## 3. PABEN Translation

The PABEN translation can be stated as follows:

| PABEN term | Physical translation in this study |
|---|---|
| Need | A continuity requirement: something must be carried forward without losing regulability |
| Context | The field condition in which the continuity requirement appears |
| Skill | The regulator or transition mechanism that performs the displacement |
| Belief | A repeatable action-context relation: do regulator while condition holds |
| Experience | Retained successful relation; a past interaction that constrains future possibilities |
| Belief-field | The set of possible continuations made available by retained relations |
| Organism | A stable inside/outside equilibrium produced by repeatable regulation |

This translation deliberately weakens the ordinary meanings of the terms. A physical Belief is not a mental belief. It is a repeatable regulatory relation. Physical Experience is not recollection. It is retained constraint: the fact that an interaction has left the system with altered future possibilities.

## 5. Physical Phenomena as Regulatory Questions

In a PABEN interpretation, physical phenomena can be approached by asking:

> What continuity is being preserved, in what context, through what repeatable displacement?

This question does not explain the mathematics of quantum mechanics, general relativity or field theory. It offers a conceptual heuristic for interpreting why certain stable structures appear.

Superposition may be read as an open field of possible continuations before a shared registration context selects one relation. Measurement may be read as the demand for a mutually readable relation between systems. Entanglement may be read as retained relational constraint after interaction. Decoherence may be read as the loss of protected internal relational openness to the surrounding context.

These formulations are interpretive. They should not be confused with formal derivations. The value of the PABEN reading would depend on whether it can later produce clearer predictions, simulations or mathematical constraints.

## 6. Relation to Existing Work

Several established research traditions make parts of this study less isolated, although none of them should be read as endorsements of PABEN.

Ilya Prigogine's work on non-equilibrium thermodynamics showed that ordered structures can arise and persist in open systems far from equilibrium. The Nobel Prize description of dissipative structures emphasizes that such order depends on exchange with the environment rather than isolation. This resonates with the PABEN idea that stable organization is regulated continuity, not static substance. See the Nobel Prize material on Prigogine and dissipative structures: [Nobel Prize in Chemistry 1977](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/1977/prigogine/facts/) and [the 1977 press release](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/1977/press-release/).

Maturana and Varela's theory of autopoiesis describes living systems as self-producing unities that maintain their own organization. PABEN differs by treating Beliefs, Experience and Needs as explicit structural domains, but the emphasis on self-maintaining organization is clearly adjacent. See *Autopoiesis and Cognition*: [Springer overview](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-94-009-8947-4).

Karl Friston's free energy principle attempts to explain how biological systems resist disorder through self-organization, circular causality and boundary-preserving dynamics. PABEN should not be collapsed into FEP, but both frameworks treat organismic stability as an active regulatory achievement. See Friston's 2012 paper: [A Free Energy Principle for Biological Systems](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3510653/).

Carlo Rovelli's relational quantum mechanics treats quantum states as relational rather than absolute. Facts arise in interactions between physical systems, and the stability of facts is connected to decoherence. This is close to the study's language of shared registration and retained relation, although PABEN adds its own action-context vocabulary. See Rovelli's overview: [The Relational Interpretation of Quantum Physics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2109.09170).

John Wheeler's "it from bit" program placed information and observation at the foundation of physical reality. This study does not claim that reality is made of bits, but it shares Wheeler's broad intuition that physical existence and registration are deeply linked. For a historical discussion, see Foschini's review: [Where the "it from bit" come from?](https://arxiv.org/abs/1306.0545).

Stuart Kauffman and later RAF theory describe how self-sustaining autocatalytic networks can arise from chemical reaction systems. This is relevant because it offers a concrete model of how repeated internal support can produce organism-like closure. See Hordijk, Hein and Steel on autocatalytic sets: [Autocatalytic Sets and the Origin of Life](https://www.mdpi.com/1099-4300/12/7/1733), and later reviews on RAF networks: [Autocatalytic Networks at the Basis of Life's Origin and Organization](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6315399/).

The common thread is not that these authors agree with one another. They do not. The common thread is that modern science repeatedly encounters systems whose identity depends on relation, boundary, exchange, history, constraint and self-maintaining organization.

## 7. Hypothesis

The study's hypothesis can be stated in a deliberately modest form:

> Stable systems emerge when a field or system discovers repeatable displacements that preserve a continuity. These repeatable displacements become retained constraints. Retained constraints form a field of available continuations. A stable organism-like structure is the inside/outside equilibrium produced by such repeatable regulation.

In PABEN language:

> A physical organism is a continuity-regime in which Context, Skill, Experience and Need have become sufficiently coupled for a Belief-field of repeatable continuations to exist.

This does not require consciousness. It does require recurrence, constraint and regulatory closure.

---

## 8. What Would Make the Hypothesis Stronger?

The hypothesis becomes stronger if it can be connected to formal or computational work. Possible next steps include:

1. Define *repeatable displacement* mathematically as a transition that preserves an invariant or restores a regulatory variable within tolerance.
2. Define *Experience* as retained constraint: a transition history that changes the probability or availability of future transitions.
3. Model a simple field of possible transitions and show how stable inside/outside structures emerge when repeatable continuity-preserving transitions are retained.
4. Compare the result with known models of autocatalytic closure, dissipative structures, Markov blankets or active inference.
5. Test whether the PABEN vocabulary adds explanatory compression or merely renames existing concepts.

The last point is essential. A sober study must allow the possibility that PABEN is only a useful metaphor unless it produces new structure, simulation results or formal constraints.

---

## 9. Main Risks

This study has several obvious risks:

- It may overextend biological vocabulary into physics.
- It may treat analogies as deeper than they are.
- It may conflate interpretation with derivation.
- It may describe known ideas in new terms without adding testable content.
- It may make the word *intelligence* too broad unless carefully constrained.

For that reason, *universal intelligence* should be understood here as a technical placeholder:

> Universal intelligence means repeatable continuity-preserving transformation, not mind, consciousness, planning or human-like cognition.

---

## 10. Conclusion

This study proposes that PABEN can be used as a cautious interpretive lens for physical organization. The central observation is that stable systems do not merely exist; they persist by regulating transformations. A continuity must be carried through change, and the transformations that can be repeated become the basis of further organization.

The proposed phrase *intelligence as repeatable displacement* should therefore be read modestly. It is not a claim that the universe thinks. It is a hypothesis that the deepest precursor of intelligence may be the ability of a system to retain and reuse transformations that preserve continuity.

If PABEN is correct, biological intelligence is not an exception to nature but a late, richly layered form of a more general pattern: unstable possibility becomes usable continuation when repeatable regulation creates an inside that can meet an outside without dissolving.
